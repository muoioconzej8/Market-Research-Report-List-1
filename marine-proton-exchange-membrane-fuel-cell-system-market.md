<p><p>The Marine Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell (PEMFC) system market features three key hydrogen storage types: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen involves storing hydrogen in high-pressure tanks, offering ease of transport and rapid refueling. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen utilizes low temperatures to store hydrogen in liquid form, maximizing energy density. Hydrides store hydrogen in solid form, allowing for safer handling and higher volumetric density, making them suitable for maritime applications requiring stable operation and efficiency.</p></p>
