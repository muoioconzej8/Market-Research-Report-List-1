<p><p>The Non-Invasive Brain Stimulation System market comprises primarily two types: Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) and Transcranial Current Stimulation (tCS) systems. TMS employs magnetic fields to stimulate neuronal activity, often used for treating depression and other neurological disorders. In contrast, tCS utilizes small electrical currents to modulate brain activity, demonstrating potential for a variety of applications, including cognitive enhancement and pain management. Both technologies offer safe, non-invasive alternatives for therapeutic and research purposes in neuroscience.</p></p>
