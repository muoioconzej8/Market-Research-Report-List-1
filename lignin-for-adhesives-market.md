<p><p>The lignin for adhesives market primarily includes three types: lignosulfonates, kraft lignin, and others. Lignosulfonates, derived from the sulfite process, are widely used for their binding properties and water solubility, making them suitable for various adhesive formulations. Kraft lignin, a byproduct of the kraft pulping process, offers excellent mechanical strength and is often used in industrial adhesives. The "others" category encompasses various lignin derivatives and blends that cater to specific applications, driving innovation in adhesive technologies.</p></p>
