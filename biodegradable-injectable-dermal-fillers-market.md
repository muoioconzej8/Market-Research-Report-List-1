<p><p>The biodegradable injectable dermal fillers market comprises two primary types: biodegradable and non-biodegradable fillers. Biodegradable fillers are composed of materials that naturally break down in the body over time, providing temporary results and reducing long-term adverse effects. In contrast, non-biodegradable fillers are made from synthetic materials that remain in the tissue, offering longer-lasting outcomes but potentially posing risks of complications. The choice between these types depends on patient preferences, desired longevity, and safety considerations in aesthetic procedures.</p></p>
