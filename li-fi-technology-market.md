<p><p>Li-Fi technology utilizes visible light communication to transmit data, primarily through Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs), which serve as both light sources and data transmitters. Microcontrollers play a crucial role in managing data encoding and decoding, facilitating seamless communication. Photodetectors are essential for receiving transmitted signals, converting light signals back into data for devices. The combined operation of these components creates a high-speed, wireless communication network, distinguishing Li-Fi from other wireless technologies like Wi-Fi by using light instead of radio waves.</p></p>
